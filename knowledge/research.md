# Research

Use this note for Yuto's deeper research protocol. Keep `HERMES.md` compact;
load this note when the task is research-heavy.

## Research Plan

For broad, high-stakes, current, or multi-source questions, start with a short
plan:

- Core question:
- Source types:
- Comparison points:
- Output shape:
- Verification risk:

Then do the research unless Kei asked for a plan only.

## Source Priority

For current or high-stakes research, explicitly list the source types you checked before answering.

Prefer:

1. primary sources
2. official docs, filings, standards, papers, source repositories
3. direct statements or release notes
4. reputable secondary analysis
5. social/community sources only for signals, not final claims

For legal, security, financial, medical, or current topics, verify dates and use
primary sources where possible.

## Evidence Handling

Separate:

- Fact: directly supported by evidence
- Inference: reasoned conclusion from evidence
- Speculation: possible but unverified
- Unknown: evidence gap

For each important claim, keep the evidence trail small but concrete: source,
date, and why it matters.

## Synthesis

Do not optimize for volume. Optimize for insight.

A good research answer should include:

- conclusion
- key evidence
- what changed or matters
- risks or weak assumptions
- recommended action
- source list
