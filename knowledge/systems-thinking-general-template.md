# Systems Thinking General Template — Before Starting Anything New

Created: 2026-05-04
Source: distilled from `Learning Systems Thinking` by Diana Montalion and adapted for Kei/Yuto as a general-purpose thinking template.

Use this before starting a new project, feature, website, AI workflow, automation, product idea, learning plan, or life/work system change.

## One-line summary

Do not ask only: “What should I do?”
Ask: “What system will this change, what behavior will it create over time, and what feedback will tell us if it is working?”

## The 10-minute version

Before doing anything new, answer these 10 questions:

1. Purpose
   - What outcome are we trying to create?
   - For whom?
   - Why now?

2. System boundary
   - What is inside the system?
   - What is outside the system?
   - What are we intentionally not solving?

3. Actors
   - Who/what participates?
   - Users, maintainers, tools, agents, teams, customers, constraints, money, time, platform rules.

4. Relationships
   - How do the parts affect each other?
   - What depends on what?
   - What could break if one part changes?

5. Current behavior
   - What is happening now?
   - Is it a one-time event or a repeating pattern?
   - What evidence do we have?

6. Structure
   - What setup makes this behavior likely?
   - Workflow, incentives, defaults, interfaces, architecture, habits, missing feedback, unclear ownership.

7. Mental models
   - What assumptions are we making?
   - What belief might be wrong?
   - What would another perspective see?

8. Feedback loops
   - What signal tells us this is working or failing?
   - How fast will we see the signal?
   - What action will we take when the signal is bad?

9. Second-order effects
   - If this works, what gets harder later?
   - If this fails, what damage does it create?
   - What hidden cost might appear after delay?

10. Smallest safe experiment
   - What is the smallest test that teaches us something real?
   - What is the stop condition?
   - What should we record for future reuse?

## The simple mental model

Events are visible. Causes are often lower.

Event:
- What just happened?

Pattern:
- Does this keep happening?

Structure:
- What setup produces the pattern?

Mental model:
- What belief keeps the structure in place?

Leverage point:
- What small structural change could shift future behavior?

## Quick checks by work type

### For product ideas

- What user behavior should change?
- What pain is repeated, not one-off?
- What metric could improve while the user experience gets worse?
- What incentive might distort the product?
- What is the smallest fake-door/prototype test?

### For websites/design

- What must the user understand first?
- Does every section support one coherent promise?
- What gets worse if we add more visual effects/content?
- What feedback proves comprehension, not just clicks?
- Can future edits stay cheap?

### For software/features

- What system behavior changes after this feature exists?
- What new coupling does it introduce?
- What failure mode appears after scale/time/team change?
- What tests/logs/metrics create feedback?
- What is the rollback path?

### For AI agents/automation

- What should the agent decide vs ask?
- What tool/action needs approval gate?
- What context should be active memory vs retrieved knowledge vs skill?
- How do we detect hallucination, drift, or silent failure?
- What audit trail exists?

### For learning/research

- What question are we trying to answer?
- What source types are needed?
- What would change our mind?
- What exercise proves we learned it?
- What should become a reusable note/template/skill?

### For personal systems

- What pattern keeps repeating?
- What structure makes it easy/hard?
- What feedback arrives too late?
- What small environmental change beats willpower?
- What metric would create unhealthy pressure?

## Red flags

Pause if any are true:

- We are fixing an event without checking the pattern.
- We are optimizing one metric without checking side effects.
- We are adding complexity without a feedback loop.
- We are relying on motivation instead of structure.
- We are using AI output without verification.
- We are changing a system we have not mapped.
- We cannot name the user, outcome, or stop condition.
- We have no rollback or recovery path.
- The change creates hidden maintenance cost.
- Success is defined only as “finished.”

## Ultra-short checklist

Before starting:

- Outcome clear?
- Boundary clear?
- Actors clear?
- Current pattern understood?
- Structure/assumptions named?
- Feedback signal defined?
- Second-order risks checked?
- Small safe experiment chosen?
- Stop/rollback condition known?
- Reusable learning capture planned?

## Teaching summary for Kei

Systems thinking is not about making everything complicated.
It is about not being fooled by the first visible symptom.

The habit is:

1. Look beyond the event.
2. Find the pattern.
3. Inspect the structure.
4. Question the mental model.
5. Change the smallest leverage point.
6. Watch feedback.
7. Learn and update the system.

This template should make new work calmer, safer, and more reusable.
